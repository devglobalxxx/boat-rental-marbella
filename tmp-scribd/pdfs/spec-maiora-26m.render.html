Section 01

A flagship day charter on the Costa del Sol

The Maiora 26m is one of the largest and most complete vessels you can charter for a single day on the Marbella coast. At 26 metres, or 90 feet, refurbished in 2023, and rated for 12 guests, she sits firmly in superyacht territory while remaining bookable for an ordinary afternoon at sea. She leaves from Puerto Banus, the most prestigious marina on the southern Spanish coast, and that combination of scale, finish and address is what makes her stand out in the BoatHire24 fleet.

What genuinely distinguishes this boat is that the experience is all-inclusive. The headline rate covers a captain and two crew, fuel for the full duration of the charter, food and an open bar, and a full inventory of water toys. There is nothing to add at the dock, no fuel surcharge waiting at the end of the day, and no separate catering bill. For groups used to the small print of yacht hire elsewhere in the Mediterranean, that single-price clarity is unusual and welcome.

She is also a yacht built for a particular kind of day. The aft cockpit is laid out for social gatherings, the interior lounges are elegant enough for a dinner or a business meeting, and the upper deck is a flybridge with a genuine 360-degree outlook over the sea and the mountains behind Marbella. Add a heated freshwater jacuzzi with panoramic sea views, and you have a vessel that works equally well as a corporate venue, a celebration platform, a family day boat and an overnight retreat. This profile sets out exactly what she offers, what a day aboard looks like, where she can take you, and how booking works.

Section 02

The builder and the marque: Maiora and the Fipa Group

The name on the hull is Maiora, and it carries real weight in the Italian yachting world. Maiora is one of the historic motor yacht marques of the Tuscan coast, a region that has been the heartland of Italian superyacht construction for decades. The yard is associated with the Fipa Group, the Viareggio-based shipbuilding group that has produced Maiora and its sister lines and that grew Maiora into a recognised name in the planing and semi-displacement motor yacht segment from roughly the 1980s onward.

Viareggio and the wider Versilia coast sit at the centre of this story. For generations the towns between Viareggio and La Spezia have been where flybridge motor yachts in the 20 to 40 metre range are designed, built and fitted out, and Maiora belongs to that lineage. The marque became known for a very specific recipe: substantial, sea-kindly hulls, generous interior volume, large social deck spaces, and a warm, craftsman-led Italian interior style rather than the cooler minimalism some rivals chase.

That heritage matters to a charter guest in concrete ways. A yacht from this tradition tends to feel solid and planted underway, carries volume well so that twelve guests are never on top of each other, and is finished to a standard meant to last and to impress. When the boat in front of you is a Maiora, you are stepping aboard a vessel from one of the established names of Italian motor yacht building, not an anonymous production hull. The 2023 refurbishment brings that pedigree up to current condition, which is why she presents so well at the dock and in the saloon.

It is worth understanding why Italian flybridge yachts in this size bracket command the reputation they do. Italy turned yacht building into an export industry, and yards along the Tyrrhenian coast learned to combine handcrafted joinery, characterful styling and serious sea-going engineering in a way that became a global benchmark. A Maiora carries that DNA: the kind of teak-and-leather warmth, the careful detailing and the considered proportion that mark a boat out as Italian the moment you step into the saloon. For a guest, the upshot is simple. You feel the quality before anyone tells you the length, and on a yacht chartered to impress clients or mark an occasion, that first impression does a great deal of the work.

Section 03

The model and the design: what a 90 ft Maiora is known for

A Maiora in the 26 metre class is, in essence, a large flybridge motor yacht, and the type has a well-understood set of strengths. The defining feature is the flybridge itself, an upper deck that delivers the 360-degree panoramic views this vessel is celebrated for. From up there the whole bay opens out: the Marbella shoreline, the marina, the open Mediterranean ahead, and the dramatic La Concha mountain rising behind the coast.

Below the flybridge, the classic Maiora layout gives you a main deck with a protected interior saloon, an aft cockpit open to the sea air, and a foredeck for sunbathing and quiet moments. The point of this arrangement is choice. On a hot Andalusian afternoon guests can spread across multiple distinct zones rather than crowding a single cockpit, which is precisely why a yacht of this size handles a full group of twelve so comfortably.

Yachts of this class are also designed for range and stability. The hull form is meant to hold a comfortable cruise in the moderate swells of the Strait approaches and the open coast, which is what allows longer runs toward Estepona, Gibraltar and beyond rather than only short hops. And the interior volume that a 90-footer carries is what makes the private cabins, two full bathrooms and elegant lounges possible, turning a day boat into a credible overnight platform. In short, this is a model built to do three things at once, cruise well, entertain well and accommodate well, and the Maiora 26m delivers all three.

Section 05

A day aboard: a charter from Puerto Banus, hour by hour

To understand the boat, picture a realistic eight-hour day. You arrive at Puerto Banus mid-morning, where the Maiora 26m is waiting on her berth among the marina's famous line-up of yachts and waterfront cafes. The captain and two crew greet you, run through a short safety briefing, and welcome you aboard with the open bar already stocked with water, soft drinks, beer, white wine and cava.

Lines come off and she eases out past the breakwater. As the marina falls behind, the coast opens up: the long sweep of Marbella beach, the villas of the Golden Mile, and La Concha standing over it all. Within twenty minutes or so the captain finds a sheltered spot to anchor in clear water. This is when the boat comes alive. The crew deploy the giant slide and the inflatable pool, the jet ski goes in, and paddleboards and snorkel gear come out. Some guests swim, some ride, some simply ease into the heated jacuzzi and watch the rest of the bay drift by.

Around the middle of the day the crew serve lunch from the included food and open bar, laid out in the aft cockpit or the interior lounge depending on the sun. Afternoon is for repositioning. The captain might lift anchor and run a little further along the coast to a second swim stop, or push toward Cabopino or Estepona for a change of scene. Late in the day, as the light turns gold, the flybridge becomes the place to be for the run home, drinks in hand and the Sierra Blanca glowing pink behind the shore. You step back onto the dock at Puerto Banus relaxed, having spent the whole day with everything taken care of. That, in a sentence, is the Maiora 26m experience.

Section 06

Itineraries and cruising grounds from Marbella

Puerto Banus is an ideal home port because it sits in the middle of a coast with options in every direction. The Maiora 26m can shape a day around the group's mood and the wind, and a few routes come up again and again.

The Golden Mile swim stop is the classic. A short cruise east of the marina brings you to the stretch of coast between Puerto Banus and Marbella town, lined with landmark hotels and villas, where the water is calm and clear and the anchorage is sheltered. It is the natural choice for a half-day or a relaxed full day that prioritises swimming and toys over distance.

Heading east toward Cabopino opens up a more natural, dune-backed shoreline beyond Marbella, with its pretty little marina and quieter anchorages, a good objective for a full day when you want to actually go somewhere rather than sit off the city beaches. Westward toward Estepona is the other direction, a longer run down a handsome coast toward one of the prettiest towns on this part of the Costa del Sol.

For groups with the time and appetite, the Maiora 26m has the range for longer runs toward Gibraltar and the Strait, where the Rock rises out of the sea and, on a clear day, the coast of Africa sits on the horizon. These bigger itineraries are best suited to the 8-hour charter or an overnight, and the captain will plan them around the weather. Wherever you point her, the appeal is the same: a stable, spacious yacht that turns the journey itself into part of the day, with the flybridge as your front-row seat to the whole Marbella coast.

One detail of local geography shapes every itinerary, and it is worth knowing. This coast is dominated by La Concha, the shell-shaped peak of the Sierra Blanca that towers over Marbella and gives the bay much of its shelter. That same mountain backdrop is why the water close in tends to stay calm on a settled summer day, and why the run home in the late afternoon, with the Sierra turning gold and then pink, is consistently the most photographed part of the charter. The prevailing summer pattern here is gentle, but conditions firm up the closer you get to the Strait of Gibraltar, where the funnelling between two continents can kick up a breeze. A good captain reads that on the morning and chooses the day's direction accordingly, which is exactly why route planning is left to the crew rather than fixed in advance.

Section 09

Seasonality and booking practicalities

The Costa del Sol earns its name. The Marbella coast enjoys one of the longest charter seasons in Europe, with warm, settled weather running from late spring through to early autumn and plenty of pleasant days either side of that core. June to September is the heart of it, when the sea is at its warmest and the swim stops, the slide and the jacuzzi are at their most inviting. This is also the busiest window, so for peak summer weekends and for any date tied to an event, the Maiora 26m should be booked well ahead.

Spring and early autumn reward the flexible. The water is still comfortable, the coast is quieter, the light is beautiful for the run home, and availability on a flagship vessel is easier to secure. These shoulder weeks suit corporate days and relaxed family charters particularly well.

A few practical points are worth knowing before you book. The yacht departs from Puerto Banus, which is straightforward to reach and a destination in its own right, with parking, restaurants and the marina promenade right at the berth. The captain plans the route on the day around the wind and sea state, so the itinerary stays comfortable whatever the conditions, and the toys are deployed at sheltered anchorages chosen by the crew. Because the boat is fully crewed and all-inclusive, your only real decisions are the date, the duration and roughly where you would like to go. For overnight or multi-day plans, raise them early so the crew can provision and route accordingly, and confirm the quote over WhatsApp through the website.
