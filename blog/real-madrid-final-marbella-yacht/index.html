The Champions League final is one of the biggest nights in football, and watching it from Marbella adds a layer of Mediterranean luxury. Whether you are a Real Madrid fan hoping for another trophy or just want a memorable evening, combining a yacht cruise from Puerto Banús with a big-screen beach club viewing is the ultimate way to experience the match. Our fleet offers boats for groups from 5 to 12 guests, with prices starting at €749 for a 2-hour charter that includes skipper, drinks, and snacks. This guide covers everything you need to plan your Real Madrid final yacht Marbella experience.

Why Watch the Final from a Yacht in Marbella?

Marbella in late spring offers warm evenings, clear skies, and a buzzing atmosphere around Puerto Banús. A yacht charter lets you escape the crowded bars while still enjoying the pre-match excitement. Cruise along the coast past landmarks like La Concha mountain and the Golden Mile, then anchor near a beach club with a giant screen. You get the best of both worlds: a private boat experience and a communal match-day vibe.

Best Beach Clubs for the Champions League Final

Several beach clubs in Marbella set up big screens for the final. Top choices include:

  • Nikki Beach Marbella – On the beachfront east of Marbella, known for its glamorous crowd and large projection screens.
  • Ocean Club Marbella – Near Puerto Banús, with a poolside screen and DJ sets before and after the match.
  • La Sala by the Sea – In Puerto Banús itself, easy walking distance from the marina.

All three require table reservations in advance for the final. Book at least two weeks ahead.

Pre-Match Cruise Itinerary

A typical evening might look like this:

  • 18:00 – Board your yacht at Puerto Banús. Meet the skipper and enjoy welcome drinks.
  • 18:15 – Depart the marina. Cruise west towards Cabopino or east towards Estepona, depending on wind and preference.
  • 19:15 – Anchor near a beach club or return to Puerto Banús for a quick walk to La Sala.
  • 20:00 – Settle in at the beach club for kick-off (usually 21:00 CEST).

If you prefer to watch from the yacht, we can arrange a longer charter and you can bring a portable screen. The 2-hour charter (€749) is ideal for the pre-match cruise only; the 4-hour option (€1,499) gives you time to cruise, anchor, and watch the first half.

Which Yacht Should You Choose?

For a group of 5–9, the Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39 are excellent choices – both depart from Puerto Banús and include a skipper, drinks, and snacks. Prices start at €749 for 2 hours. For larger groups up to 12, consider the Mangusta 80 (€4,719 for 4 hours, includes a jet ski). All charters include VAT at 21%, so there are no surprises.

What’s Included in Your Charter

Every booking includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, and safety equipment. You don’t need to bring anything except swimwear and sunscreen. The skipper can also recommend the best spots for anchoring near the beach clubs.

Book Early – Demand is High

The Champions League final is one of the busiest nights of the year in Marbella. Yacht charters and beach club tables sell out weeks in advance. We recommend booking at least three weeks ahead, especially if you want a specific boat. Contact us via WhatsApp for availability and to customise your package. Frequently asked questions

What is the best way to watch the Champions League final in Marbella?

Many fans combine a pre-match yacht cruise from Puerto Banús with a big-screen beach club viewing. You can cruise along the coast for 2 hours, then head to a beach club like Nikki Beach or Ocean Club for the match. Alternatively, watch from the yacht itself if you have a small group and a TV setup – ask us about options.

Can I watch the Real Madrid final directly from the yacht?

Our yachts are not equipped with large screens, but you can bring a tablet or small monitor for a private viewing. For the full atmosphere, we recommend a beach club with a giant screen. We can arrange a charter that drops you at a beach club in time for kick-off.

How much does a yacht charter cost for the final?

Tier A boats like the Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39 start at €749 for 2 hours (includes skipper, drinks, snacks, and VAT). For longer charters, the 8-hour price is €2,299. Larger yachts like the Mangusta 80 start at €4,719 for 4 hours. All prices include VAT.

Which beach clubs in Marbella show the Champions League final?

Popular options include Nikki Beach Marbella, Ocean Club Marbella, and La Sala by the Sea in Puerto Banús. These venues typically have giant screens and a lively atmosphere. We recommend booking a table in advance as they fill up quickly for big matches.

What is included in the yacht charter price?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, safety equipment, and Spanish IVA (21%). There are no hidden fees. You just need to bring your own swimwear and sunscreen.

Can I combine a yacht cruise with a beach club visit?

Yes, absolutely. Many guests book a 2-hour pre-match cruise along the coast, then dock at Puerto Banús or Marbella Marina and walk to a nearby beach club. We can help coordinate timing so you arrive with 30–45 minutes to spare before kick-off.

How many people can I bring on the yacht for the final?

Capacity varies by yacht. The Astondoa 40 takes up to 9 passengers, the Azimut 39 up to 11, and larger boats like the Mangusta 80 up to 12. For groups of 5 or fewer, the speedboat or Dubhe are good options. Check our fleet page for exact capacities.

Is it possible to book a yacht for just the match duration?

Yes, but note that our minimum charter is 2 hours. For a 90-minute match plus pre-match buildup, a 2-hour charter is ideal if you watch from a beach club. If you want to stay on the yacht for the match, consider a 4-hour charter to include cruising before and after.
