Marbella's waters are a prime destination for big game tuna fishing. The Alboran Sea, just off the Costa del Sol, hosts bluefin tuna runs from June to October, with fish often exceeding 50 kg. Our private charters depart from Puerto Banús and include a licensed skipper, all fishing gear, bait, drinks, and snacks. Whether you're an experienced angler or a first-timer, our boats offer a comfortable, professional platform for a day on the water.

Why Marbella for Big Game Tuna Fishing?

The Mediterranean's bluefin tuna migrate through the Strait of Gibraltar into the Alboran Sea, making Marbella a strategic launch point. Water temperatures in summer reach 24–26°C, and the continental shelf drops quickly, bringing tuna within 5–10 nautical miles of the coast. The season peaks from July to September, when catches of 60–100 kg are common. Local skippers know the hotspots near Cabopino, Cala del Faro, and Estepona.

Typical Day on the Water

Your charter starts at Puerto Banús marina. After a safety briefing, the skipper heads out to the fishing grounds, usually 5–15 nautical miles offshore. Trolling with live bait or lures is the standard technique. The skipper will rig the lines and coach you on fighting the fish. Catches are cleaned and packed on request. You can also swim or relax between action. Most charters last 4–8 hours, but shorter 2-hour trips are available for a taste of the sport.

Season & Conditions

Bluefin tuna season runs June–October. Peak months are July, August, and September. Early morning departures (7–8 AM) often yield the best action. Sea conditions are generally calm in summer, with swells under 1 m. If the wind picks up, the skipper may adjust the route or reschedule. We recommend booking in advance during peak season as demand is high.

Frequently asked questions

What is included in a big game tuna fishing charter in Marbella?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, insurance, safety equipment, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, and light snacks. Fishing gear, bait, and tackle are also included. The Spanish IVA (21%) is included in the price.

Which boats are available for tuna fishing?

We offer the Astondoa 40 (12.5m, 9 pax), Azimut 39 (12.5m, 11 pax), and Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) for fishing charters. All depart from Puerto Banús. Other boats may be available on request.

Do I need a fishing license?

No. The charter includes a temporary fishing license for all passengers, arranged by the skipper. You just show up and fish.

What is the best time of year for big game tuna fishing in Marbella?

Bluefin tuna season runs from June to October, with peak catches from July through September. Water temperatures then reach 22–26°C, bringing larger fish closer to the coast.

How far do we go from the coast?

Depending on conditions and tuna location, you may travel 5–15 nautical miles from Marbella. The skipper will head to known fishing grounds in the Alboran Sea, often near Cabopino or Estepona.

Can we keep the fish we catch?

Yes. The skipper can help clean and pack your catch for transport. Note that there are legal size limits and quotas for bluefin tuna, which the skipper will ensure are respected.

What size of tuna can we expect?

Bluefin tuna in the Alboran Sea typically range from 30 kg to over 100 kg. Catches of 50–80 kg are common during peak season. The gear provided is heavy-duty to handle these fish.
