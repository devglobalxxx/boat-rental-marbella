New Year's Eve on a yacht in Marbella means watching the Puerto Banús fireworks from the water with a glass of cava in hand, skipper at the helm, and your group spread across the deck instead of squeezed into a beach-club queue. Charters start at €749 for two hours on our Tier A boats — skipper, drinks and 21% IVA included — and scale up to the 24-metre Mangusta 80 for groups of twelve. This page covers what NYE looks like on board, which boat suits your party, what it costs, and how to lock in the date before mid-December when the diary closes.

Why spend NYE on the water in Marbella

The Costa del Sol is one of the few places in Europe where 31 December can still be spent comfortably outdoors. Air sits between 13°C and 17°C through the afternoon, the sea holds at 15-16°C, and the Sierra Blanca shelters the coast from northerly wind — so most evenings are flat or gently rippled. Add the Puerto Banús municipal fireworks at midnight, private displays along the Golden Mile and the lights of Marbella town behind La Concha, and you have a 360° view no land venue can match. Boat Rental Marbella runs all NYE charters from Puerto Banús with our own fleet — no third-party operators.

The fireworks viewing line

The official municipal display launches from the Puerto Banús breakwater shortly after the midnight bells. Your skipper anchors 200-400 metres offshore opposite the marina entrance, which puts the boat directly under the burst pattern and clear of the harbour's no-go zone. From the same anchor you also see private fireworks from beach clubs in Río Verde and villas above the Golden Mile, and the marina itself stays lit until around 03:00. Most skippers cut engines for 20 minutes either side of midnight so the deck stays silent for the toast.

Choosing the right boat for your group

For couples and groups up to 6, the Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39 hit the sweet spot — sheltered cockpit, heated saloon, easy to anchor close to the fireworks line. Eight to twelve guests should step up to the Azimut 58 Flybridge or one of the Mangusta 80s for the indoor lounge and dual decks; the Mangusta also throws in a jet ski for the daytime hours if you start earlier. Larger celebrations of ten to twelve who want a sit-down dinner on board are best on the Canados 86 or Ferretti 94, both 26-29 m with a full galley. Browse the entire fleet for deck plans.

Three popular NYE itineraries

  • Midnight Express (21:00-01:00, 4h) — Tier A boat, board with cava, cruise to Cala del Faro, drift back to the fireworks line by 23:30, anchor for midnight, return 01:00.
  • Sunset to Fireworks (17:30-22:30, 5h) — catch the 18:10 sunset off Marbella Marina, dinner on board, fireworks-free early return for families.
  • Full NYE (20:00-02:00, 6h on Mangusta 80 or Ferretti 94) — DJ on deck, catered tasting menu, midnight at anchor, slow cruise back along the Golden Mile lights.

What to wear and bring

Temperatures drop noticeably once you leave the breakwater. Plan for 10-12°C at midnight with a light 5-10 kt breeze. Bring a windproof jacket, a warm layer, a hat and closed shoes with non-marking soles. Heels are fine inside the saloon but awkward on a wet deck. Phones and cameras work well from the flybridge; if you want long-exposure fireworks shots, a small tripod fits any of our boats. Glassware and crockery are provided — no need to bring anything edible or alcoholic, though you are welcome to.

Weather, safety and contingency

We track AEMET forecasts daily from 24 December onwards. If wind exceeds 15 knots or swell tops 1.5 m, the skipper shortens the offshore leg and anchors closer to the harbour wall — the fireworks view is unaffected. In the rare case of a full small-craft warning, you can rebook to any night in January or take a 100% credit. All boats carry full lifejacket sets, EPIRB and twin VHF, and skippers hold a Spanish PER licence or higher.

Frequently asked questions

Can I see the Puerto Banús fireworks from the boat?

Yes. The municipal display launches from the Puerto Banús breakwater shortly after midnight. Your skipper anchors a few hundred metres offshore opposite the marina, giving a clear, crowd-free view across the water. From there you also catch fireworks set off privately from beach clubs along the Golden Mile and from villas above Río Verde.

How much does a New Year's Eve charter cost?

Tier A boats (Astondoa 40, Azimut 39) start at €749 for two hours and €2,299 for eight hours, skipper, drinks and 21% IVA included. The Mangusta 80 runs €4,719 for the four-hour minimum with a free jet ski. Larger flybridges (Ferretti 94, Canados 86, Azimut 58) are priced on request via WhatsApp.

What time should we depart on 31 December?

Most groups choose a 21:00-01:00 window so you are anchored offshore for the midnight countdown. Earlier 18:00-22:00 slots work well for families who want sunset (around 18:10), the early harbour lights and a calmer return. We can also run shorter 2-hour midnight-only trips on Tier A boats.

What is included in the NYE price?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, insurance, safety equipment and Spanish IVA at 21%. Onboard you get water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava and light snacks. For NYE we add extra cava for the midnight toast at no charge. Catered dinners, DJs and premium spirits can be arranged — ask when booking.

How cold is it on the water on New Year's Eve?

Air temperatures in Marbella on 31 December typically sit between 13°C and 17°C during the day, dropping to 10-12°C by midnight. Sea temperature averages 15-16°C. The flybridge boats have heated saloons, but bring a windproof jacket, hat and closed shoes — it always feels cooler offshore than on the marina.

How many guests can come on board?

Capacity ranges from 5 on the Dubhe up to 12 on the Mangusta 80, Azimut 58 Flybridge, Canados 86 and Ferretti 94. The Azimut 39 carries 11. For NYE we recommend the larger flybridges so the group can stay warm inside between the deck moments. See the full fleet for layouts.

Will the sea be rough on 31 December?

The Costa del Sol is sheltered by the Sierra Blanca, and winter evenings are often glassy with a light Levante or Poniente of 5-10 knots. If forecasts exceed 15 knots your skipper will shorten the offshore leg and anchor closer to Puerto Banús. We monitor AEMET daily in the week before and re-route or rebook if conditions turn.

When should I book a New Year's Eve charter in Marbella?

NYE is the single most requested night of the year — most boats are reserved by mid-December and the Mangusta 80 typically goes first. Message us on WhatsApp as soon as your dates are confirmed; we hold the slot with a 30% deposit and the balance settles on the day of departure.
