Why October Works for a Marbella Yacht Charter

October in Marbella delivers 24°C air temperatures, 8–10 hours of daily sunshine, and sea conditions that are flat-calm more often than not. The prevailing westerly breeze stays light (10–15 knots), and swell rarely exceeds half a metre. That means no chop, no white-knuckle rides — just smooth cruising from Puerto Banús to the coves east of Marbella or west to Estepona. The water is warm enough for swimming until late afternoon, and the beaches along the Golden Mile are empty enough that you’ll feel like you have the coast to yourself.

Best October Destinations from Puerto Banús

With calm seas and shorter daylight (sunset around 19:30), October is perfect for half-day or full-day itineraries. Here are our top picks:

  • Cala del Faro (20 min east) — A quiet cove with clear water, ideal for swimming and snorkelling. Moor up and enjoy the view of Marbella’s coastline.
  • Cala Cortés (30 min east) — A small, sheltered beach near Cabopino. Great for a relaxed lunch stop.
  • Estepona (12 NM west, 1 hour) — The old town marina offers a charming stop for tapas. The sea is usually flat on this route in October.
  • Sotogrande (20 NM west, 1.5 hours) — For a full-day trip, head to the luxury marina; the beach clubs are quieter in October.
  • Marbella Marina (15 min east) — A short hop from Puerto Banús, ideal for a sunset cocktail on the water.

What to Wear and Bring in October

Daytime is warm enough for shorts and a T-shirt, but bring a light jacket or sweater for the evening cruise back to port. The sun is still strong, so sunscreen (SPF 50) and sunglasses are essential. Swimwear and a towel are a must — you’ll want to jump in. If you’re prone to seasickness, note that October seas are generally calm, but a mild antihistamine or ginger tablets are a sensible precaution.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is October a good time for a yacht charter in Marbella?

Yes. October is shoulder season with 22°C sea temperature, 24°C air, and 8–10 hours of sun. The water is still warm for swimming, anchorages are uncrowded, and rates are lower than summer. Most days have calm seas, making it ideal for cruising the Costa del Sol.

Which boats are available for October charters from Puerto Banús?

Our full fleet operates year-round from Puerto Banús. Popular October choices include the Azimut 39 (11 pax, €749/2h), Mangusta 80 (12 pax, €4,719/4h with jet ski), and Fairline Targa 12m (10 pax). All depart from Puerto Banús marina.

What is included in the charter price?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, safety equipment, and Spanish IVA (21%). No hidden costs. Tier B boats also include a jet ski.

Can we swim and snorkel in October?

Absolutely. The sea temperature in October averages 22°C, similar to June. Popular spots like Cala del Faro, Cala Cortés, and the coast near Cabopino offer clear water for swimming, snorkelling, and paddleboarding. We provide snorkel gear on request.

How do October prices compare to summer?

October is shoulder season, so demand is lower. Tier A boats (Astondoa 40, Azimut 39) start at €749 for 2 hours or €2,299 for a full 8-hour day. These rates are typically 15–20% below July–August peak. Other boats are priced on request via WhatsApp.

What destinations are best in October from Marbella?

Calm seas make it easy to reach Estepona (12 NM west), Sotogrande (20 NM west), or Cabopino (8 NM east). For shorter trips, Cala del Faro and Cala Cortés are 20–30 minutes from Puerto Banús. Marbella Marina is 15 minutes east.

How many guests can we bring on a charter?

Our largest boats, including the Ferretti 94, Canados 86, and Mangusta 80, carry up to 12 guests. The Azimut 39 holds 11, and the Astondoa 40 holds 9. Smaller options like the Mariah SX21 or Speedboat take 5–6 guests. Check the fleet page for exact capacities.
