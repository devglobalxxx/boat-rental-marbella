A birthday yacht charter in Marbella starts at €749 for 2 hours on an Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39, with a licensed skipper, drinks, cava and 21% Spanish VAT already included. For groups of 10-12 chasing a statement day, a Mangusta 80 with a free jet ski is €4,719 for 4 hours. Every boat in our Puerto Banús fleet is bookable for birthdays, and the only honest question is which one fits your guest count, budget and vibe.

The short answer: pick by group size

Spanish law caps guests at 12 plus crew, so birthdays naturally split into three brackets. Five to six guests fit on the Dubhe, Mariah SX21, Red Tide or Speedboat — fast, intimate, and from €749/2h on similar Tier A boats. Seven to ten guests are the Astondoa 40, Bandido, Lagoon 380, Fairline Targa 12m or Pershing 46 sweet spot. Eleven to twelve guests demand the Azimut 39, Azimut 58 Flybridge, Mangusta 80, K80, Canados 86 or Ferretti 94.

Don't oversize the boat. A 9-guest birthday on a 24m Mangusta feels empty; the same nine on an Astondoa 40 feels like a proper party with everyone in conversation distance.

What's included on every birthday charter

  • Licensed Spanish skipper (RYA or equivalent qualified)
  • Fuel for a typical 15-25 NM coastal cruise
  • Bottled water, soft drinks, local beer, white wine and Spanish cava
  • Light snacks (olives, crisps, nuts, fresh fruit)
  • Snorkel masks, swim ladder, floating mats
  • Full insurance and safety equipment
  • Spanish IVA (21%) — no surprises at the dock

What's not included: champagne upgrades, hot catering, jet skis (except free on the Mangusta 80 package), paddleboards, seabobs and dockside cake delivery. All can be added — ask on WhatsApp when you book.

Cake, decor, music: the birthday extras

The four most-asked extras for birthday charters:

  • Cake. Bring your own from any Marbella patisserie — Lúa, Mercado Gourmet, or supermarket if you're keeping it simple. We provide knife, plates and a small fridge. Order a cake under 25cm diameter so it fits the saloon counter.
  • Decorations. Paper bunting, helium balloons and a 'Happy Birthday' banner are welcome. No glitter, no confetti, no foil flutter — Spanish maritime law protects the sea and we will not depart with banned items on board.
  • Music. Every boat has Bluetooth speakers. Bring a phone with your Spotify playlist downloaded offline (mobile signal drops 3 NM offshore). Volume must drop after 22:00 to respect coastal residents.
  • Photographer. A 2-hour on-board photographer in Marbella runs €250-€400. We can recommend two we trust — ask on WhatsApp.

Best route for a birthday day

The classic Marbella birthday route runs west from Puerto Banús along the Golden Mile, past Marbella Club and Puente Romano, with anchor stops at Cala del Faro (sheltered, sandy bottom, 5m clear water) and Río Verde for the swim segment. Sunset birthdays push further west toward Cala Cortés or Estepona, with the sun dropping behind La Concha mountain at roughly 21:30 in July and 20:00 in May.

For 4-hour-plus charters, ambitious groups push to Sotogrande (16 NM west) for lunch on a moored buoy, then cruise back. The Mangusta 80 covers it comfortably; smaller boats are better staying within 10 NM.

All prices include skipper, fuel, drinks and VAT. We don't add 'fuel surcharges' or 'cleaning fees' at the dock — what you see is what you pay.

When to book your Marbella birthday charter

July and August Saturdays sell out 3-4 weeks ahead. June and September weekends need 2 weeks' notice. May and October weekdays often have same-week availability. Sunset slots (17:00-20:00) sell faster than midday slots because there are fewer of them per day. Weekdays unlock the best skipper picks and easier pontoon access in Puerto Banús.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a birthday yacht charter in Marbella cost?

Entry-level birthday charters on the Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39 start at €749 for 2 hours, including a licensed skipper, fuel, drinks (beer, white wine, cava, soft drinks), light snacks and 21% Spanish VAT. An 8-hour day on the same boats is €2,299. For 12-guest superyacht birthdays on a Mangusta 80, prices begin at €4,719 for 4 hours with a free jet ski. Larger yachts like the Ferretti 94 or Canados 86 are quoted on request via WhatsApp.

How many guests can I bring on a birthday charter?

Spanish charter law caps guests at 12 plus crew, regardless of boat size. Our smaller boats fit 5-10 people: Dubhe (5), Mariah SX21 (5), Red Tide (6), Speedboat (6), Bandido (8), Astondoa 40 (9), Lagoon 380 (10), Fairline Targa 12m (10) and Pershing 46 (10). For a full group of 12 you'll want the Azimut 58 Flybridge, Mangusta 80, K80, Canados 86 or Ferretti 94. We never overload boats.

Which boat is best for a birthday party?

For 6-9 guests on a budget, the Astondoa 40 at €749/2h is the sweet spot — sundeck, bathing platform and full bar. For 10-12 guests who want flybridge sunbathing and shade, choose the Azimut 58 Flybridge or Pershing 46. For a statement birthday with jet ski included, the Mangusta 80 at €4,719/4h is hard to beat. Catamaran fans should pick the Lagoon 380 for its stable trampoline lounging.

Can we bring a birthday cake, decorations and our own music?

Yes to all three. Bring any cake (we provide a knife, plates and a fridge), biodegradable decorations like paper bunting or balloons (no glitter or confetti — it pollutes the sea), and your own Spotify playlist via Bluetooth. The skipper can also dim lights and slow to a drift for the cake moment. We supply ice buckets, glassware and bottle openers.

What time of day works best for a birthday on the water?

Two windows dominate. Midday 12:00-16:00 gives you 25-28°C, swim stops at Cala del Faro and Río Verde and Instagram-perfect light. Sunset 17:00-20:00 (later in July-August) is cooler, golden, and ideal for a dinner-style party with cava on the bow as Marbella's skyline glows. Mornings 09:00-12:00 are calmest if anyone gets seasick — flat water, no chop.

Where do birthday charters in Marbella depart from?

Every boat in our fleet departs Puerto Banús, the marina five minutes west of Marbella centre. Meet your skipper at the pontoon 15 minutes before departure with ID for the lead booker. Parking is paid underground at Cristamar (€2-3/hour). Taxis from Marbella Old Town run €12-15. From Málaga airport, allow 50 minutes by car or pre-booked transfer.

Can we swim and use water toys during the birthday charter?

Absolutely — swimming is the highlight for most groups. Skippers anchor in sheltered coves like Cala del Faro, Cala Cortés or off the Golden Mile in 4-6m of clear water. All charters include snorkels, a swim ladder and floating mats. The Mangusta 80 birthday package includes a free jet ski (licence or skipper-assisted). Paddleboards and seabobs can be added on request.

What if the weather is bad on my birthday?

If our skipper deems winds above 20 knots or 1.5m+ swell unsafe to depart, we reschedule free of charge to any date in the same season, or refund 100% if you can't reschedule. Light cloud and 15-knot breezes don't cancel a charter — Marbella averages 320 sunny days a year and June-September almost never sees a no-sail day. We make the call by 09:00 on the day.
