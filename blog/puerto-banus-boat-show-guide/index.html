The Puerto Banús boat show is the Costa del Sol’s premier yachting event, drawing visitors from across Europe to the glitzy marina of Puerto Banús. Whether you’re a seasoned yacht owner or a first-time charterer, this guide covers everything: show dates, how to visit, demo sails, and on-site charters from our fleet. Prices start at €749 for 2 hours on a Tier A boat, with all charters including a skipper, drinks, and snacks. Read on to plan your day at the show and secure a spot on the water.

Puerto Banús Boat Show Dates and Location

The Puerto Banús International Boat Show is held annually in late April or early May, typically spanning 4–5 days. The venue is the marina itself, with exhibitors lining the docks between the luxury boutiques and waterfront restaurants. Exact dates are confirmed a few months prior, so check the official site or our blog for updates. The show is free to enter, with VIP tickets available for premium access. Puerto Banús is easily reached from Málaga Airport (45 minutes) or Gibraltar Airport (1 hour).

What to Expect at the Boat Show

Expect to see a wide range of vessels, from small speedboats to superyachts over 30 metres. Exhibitors include builders like Azimut, Ferretti, and Mangusta, as well as charter companies and marine service providers. The atmosphere is lively but relaxed, with plenty of opportunities to step aboard yachts, talk to experts, and even book a demo sail. Our fleet will be on display at the marina, and we’re offering on-site charters throughout the show.

Demo Sails and On-Site Charters

One of the best ways to experience a yacht is to take it out on the water. We offer demo sails on several of our boats during the show, including the Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) and the Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m, 12 pax). These are short trips (typically 1–2 hours) led by a licensed skipper, with drinks and snacks included. For a longer experience, book a full charter: Tier A boats like the Astondoa 40 and Azimut 39 start at €749 for 2 hours or €2,299 for 8 hours. The Mangusta 80 tier starts at €4,719 for 4 hours and includes a free jet ski. All bookings include fuel, insurance, and Spanish IVA. Contact us via WhatsApp to arrange your slot.

Tips for Visiting the Boat Show

Arrive early to avoid crowds, especially on weekends. Wear comfortable shoes—you’ll be walking along docks and boarding yachts. Bring sunscreen and a light jacket, as the sea breeze can be cool even in spring. If you plan to charter, book in advance, as availability fills quickly during the show. Parking near Puerto Banús is limited; consider a taxi or shuttle from Marbella. For dining, the marina offers everything from tapas bars to Michelin-starred restaurants.

Chartering After the Show

The boat show is a great time to secure a charter for later in the season. We offer flexible bookings for day trips, sunset cruises, or multi-day itineraries along the Costa del Sol. Popular destinations include Cabopino, Estepona, and Sotogrande. All charters include a licensed skipper, fuel, drinks, and snacks. Check our full fleet page for more details and to book online.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Puerto Banús boat show?

The Puerto Banús International Boat Show typically takes place in late April or early May each year. Exact dates are announced a few months ahead, so check the official site or our blog for updates. The show runs for 4–5 days, including weekends, with extended hours for visitors.

Is entry to the boat show free?

Yes, general admission to the exhibition area at Puerto Banús is free. VIP tickets, offering access to exclusive lounges, private viewings, and networking events, are available for purchase. Check the official boat show website for ticket prices and availability.

Can I book a charter during the boat show?

Absolutely. We offer on-site charters from our fleet at Puerto Banús. You can book a demo sail on boats like the Mangusta 80 or Azimut 39. Tier A charters start at €749 for 2 hours or €2,299 for 8 hours, including skipper, drinks, and snacks. Contact us via WhatsApp to arrange a slot.

What boats are available for demo sails?

During the show, we offer demo sails on select yachts from our fleet, including the Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax), Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m, 12 pax), and Astondoa 40 (12.5m, 9 pax). Availability is limited, so book early. Each demo sail includes a licensed skipper and refreshments.

How do I get to Puerto Banús for the boat show?

Puerto Banús is about a 45-minute drive from Málaga Airport and 1 hour from Gibraltar Airport. Taxis and private transfers are readily available. Parking near the marina is limited, so consider public transport or a pre-booked shuttle. The show is walkable from Marbella's Golden Mile.

Are children allowed on charter boats during the show?

Yes, children are welcome on our charters, subject to safety regulations. Life jackets are provided for all ages. Some boats have a minimum age for certain activities, but generally, families can enjoy demo sails. Contact us for specific boat recommendations for your group.

What is included in a charter booking?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, and safety equipment. Spanish IVA (21%) is included in the price. The Mangusta 80 tier also includes a free jet ski. No hidden fees.

Can I charter a boat for a private event during the show?

Yes, our boats are available for private events, including corporate gatherings, birthdays, or sunset cruises. The Ferretti 94 (29m, 12 pax) and Canados 86 (26m, 12 pax) are ideal for entertaining. Contact us via WhatsApp to discuss your requirements and availability.
