By Andra Kiirkivi Founder & CEO · Boat Rental Marbella · Updated 20 May 2026 · Reviewed by local Marbella skippers

If you’re looking for a fast, fun, and affordable way to get on the water in Marbella, our 7m speedboat might be exactly what you need. This is a straight-talking review of what it’s actually like onboard – no fluff, just the facts from a real charter experience. We’ll cover the boat itself, the route, the vibe, and who it’s best suited for.

First Impressions: The Boat

Our 7m speedboat is a sporty open-bow design, white hull, blue upholstery. It’s not a luxury yacht – it’s a day boat built for speed and sun. There’s a small windscreen, a helm station with basic electronics, and seating for six across two bench seats. The foredeck has a sunpad area for lounging. No cabin, no toilet, no galley – just a cooler with drinks and snacks provided. The boat feels solid and well-maintained, with a Yamaha outboard that pushes it to around 35 knots in good conditions.

Departure from Puerto Banús

All our speedboat charters depart from Puerto Banús, Marbella’s most famous marina. You meet the skipper at the appointed time, board at the pontoon, and get a quick safety briefing. The skipper will ask about your preferences – fast cruising, relaxed sightseeing, swimming stops. Within five minutes you’re motoring past the superyacht row, heading out past the breakwater into the Mediterranean. The sound of the engine changes as you open the throttle, and the bow lifts. It’s an immediate adrenaline hit.

Onboard Experience: Speed, Sound & Comfort

At cruising speed (around 25 knots) the ride is firm but not jarring – the hull handles chop well for a 7m boat. At full throttle you’ll feel the wind and spray; bring a windbreaker even on sunny days. The seats are comfortable for up to an hour at a time, but there’s no shade beyond the small windscreen. The skipper will adjust speed to suit the group. The sound level is loud enough that conversation requires raised voices, but not deafening. It’s part of the experience – raw and exciting.

Typical Route & Swim Stops

A common route goes west from Puerto Banús towards Marbella Marina, then on to Cala del Faro – a small cove with clear water and rocky cliffs. Alternatively, heading east takes you to Cabopino and the Artola Dunes. The skipper will anchor in a calm spot for swimming. The boat has a telescopic ladder at the stern for easy reboarding. Snorkelling gear isn’t provided, so bring your own if you want to explore underwater. Typical swim stops last 20–40 minutes depending on your itinerary.

Who Is This Boat For?

This speedboat is ideal for small groups of friends, couples, or families with older children (life jackets are available for all ages). It’s not a luxury yacht experience – it’s about the thrill of the ride, the sun, and the sea. If you want a quiet, lounging day with a full galley and air conditioning, look at our larger motor yachts like the Azimut 58 or Mangusta 80. If you want a fast, affordable half-day charter with real speed, this is your boat.

Pricing & Booking

Pricing for the speedboat is on request – there’s no fixed rate because each charter is tailored to duration and route. Contact us via WhatsApp for a quote. The price includes the skipper, fuel, insurance, drinks (water, soft drinks, beer, wine, cava), light snacks, and the 21% Spanish VAT. No hidden fees. A typical half-day (4 hours) is very competitive compared to larger boats in our fleet. For comparison, our Tier A boats (Astondoa 40, Azimut 39) start at €749 for 2 hours – the speedboat is significantly less.

What’s Not Included

No meals, no snorkel gear, no towels, no toilet. Plan ahead: use the marina facilities before departure, bring sunscreen, a towel, and a waterproof bag for valuables. The skipper will advise on the best public stops if needed.

Final Verdict

The 7m speedboat delivers exactly what it promises: a fast, fun, no-frills day on the water in Marbella. It’s not for everyone – if you want luxury, book a yacht charter. If you want speed, sun, and swim stops at a fair price, this is an excellent choice. The skipper makes it safe and easy, and departing from Puerto Banús adds instant glamour. For a 3–4 hour adventure, it’s hard to beat.

Frequently Asked Questions

How fast does the speedboat go?

Our 7m speedboat reaches around 35 knots depending on load and sea conditions. It’s quick enough for a thrill but comfortable for cruising – not a race boat.

Can I drive the speedboat myself?

No. A licensed skipper is included with every charter. You can ask to take the wheel under supervision if conditions allow, but the skipper remains in control.

Is there a toilet onboard?

No, the speedboat has no cabin or toilet. We plan stops at beaches or marinas with public facilities, so it’s manageable for a half-day trip.

What’s included in the price?

The charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, insurance, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, and Spanish VAT (21%). No hidden extras.

Where can we go on the speedboat?

Typical routes head east to Cabopino or west to Marbella Marina and Cala del Faro. The skipper can tailor the trip to your preferences, weather permitting.

How many people fit on the speedboat?

Maximum 6 guests plus the skipper. It’s best for 4–5 adults for comfort, though 6 works for a short trip. Life jackets are provided for all.

Can we swim and snorkel?

Yes. The skipper will stop at calm coves like Cala del Faro or Cala Cortés for swimming. We provide a ladder for easy reboarding.

How do I book the speedboat?

Pricing is on request – message us on WhatsApp. We’ll confirm availability, give you a fixed price, and reserve the boat. A deposit secures the booking.
