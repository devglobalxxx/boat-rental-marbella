Do You Need a Fishing License for a Marbella Yacht Charter?

Under Spanish law, recreational fishing from a private vessel requires a license if you fish independently. However, when you charter a boat with a licensed skipper who supervises the activity, guests are generally exempt from holding individual licenses. The skipper assumes responsibility for compliance with local regulations, including catch limits and protected species. Always confirm with your skipper before departure, but for most rod-and-line fishing trips from Marbella, no license is needed.

Fishing Regulations on the Costa del Sol

Spain enforces minimum size limits for common species: sea bass (36 cm), sea bream (23 cm), and mackerel (no minimum but quotas apply). Tuna and swordfish have strict bag limits. Fishing is prohibited within 500 metres of bathing beaches and in marine reserves. Your skipper knows the legal zones off Marbella, Estepona, and Sotogrande. Stick to rod-and-line—nets and spears are not allowed on our charters.

What to Bring for Your Fishing Trip

We do not supply fishing gear. Bring your own rods, reels, and tackle. If you need rental equipment, contact us in advance. Sunscreen, a hat, and polarised sunglasses are essential. The Mediterranean sun is strong even in spring. Our boats provide water, soft drinks, beer, and light snacks. For longer trips, you can bring your own food.

Best Times and Species for Fishing in Marbella

Spring (April–June) and autumn (September–November) offer the best fishing for sea bass, bream, and mackerel. Summer (July–August) brings warmer water and the chance of tuna, though sea conditions are usually calm. Winter sees fewer tourists and good bottom fishing for grouper and snapper. Water temperatures range from 14°C in January to 26°C in August.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a Spanish fishing license to fish on a yacht charter in Marbella?

For recreational rod-and-line fishing from a private charter in Marbella, guests do not need a Spanish recreational fishing license, provided the fishing is done under the supervision of the licensed skipper and within legal limits. However, if you plan to fish independently from shore or without a charter, a license is required. Always confirm with your skipper before the trip.

What type of fishing is allowed on Marbella yacht charters?

Rod-and-line fishing is the standard method allowed on our charters. Spearfishing, nets, or traps are not permitted. The skipper will advise on catch-and-release practices and size limits. You can fish for species like sea bass, bream, mackerel, and occasionally tuna depending on the season and location.

Can I keep the fish I catch on a charter?

Yes, you can keep fish that meet the legal minimum size limits set by Spanish regulations. The skipper will help identify legal catches. Common keepers include sea bass (minimum 36 cm) and bream. For larger species like tuna, specific quotas apply. We recommend catch-and-release for sustainability.

Which boats are best for fishing in Marbella?

Larger boats like the Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) or Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m, 12 pax) offer stable platforms and ample deck space for fishing. For smaller groups, the Astondoa 40 (12.5m, 9 pax) or the speedboat (7m, 6 pax) work well. All depart from Puerto Banús.

Do you provide fishing rods and tackle?

No, we do not supply fishing gear as standard. You are welcome to bring your own rods, reels, and tackle. If you need rental equipment, let us know in advance and we can arrange it through a local supplier. The skipper can advise on suitable gear for the area.

What is the best time of year for fishing in Marbella?

Fishing is good year-round, but spring (April–June) and autumn (September–November) offer the best conditions for species like sea bass and bream. Summer (July–August) is popular for mackerel and tuna, though sea conditions are calmer. Winter sees fewer tourists and good bottom fishing.

Are there any restrictions on fishing near Marbella?

Yes, fishing is prohibited in marine protected areas like Cabo de Gata or within 500 metres of bathing beaches. Your skipper knows the legal zones. We fish in open waters off the coast of Marbella, Estepona, and sometimes near Sotogrande. Always follow the skipper’s instructions.

How do I book a fishing charter from Puerto Banús?

You can book directly through our website or contact us via WhatsApp. Choose your boat based on group size and budget. Tier A boats start at €749 for 2 hours. For Tier B boats (Mangusta 80), a 4-hour minimum applies. All bookings include a licensed skipper, fuel, drinks, and snacks.
