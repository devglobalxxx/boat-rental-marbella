Average Temperatures and Sea Conditions

June in Marbella sees daytime highs of 30°C and lows around 20°C after sunset. The sea surface temperature averages 22°C – warm enough for extended swimming without a wetsuit. Humidity is moderate (60–65%), and the UV index hits 9, so sunscreen is essential. Rainfall is negligible: typically less than 5 mm across the month, often falling as a brief evening shower that clears before morning.

The prevailing wind is the Poniente, blowing from the west at 10–15 knots. This produces a slight sea state (Douglas scale 1–2) with waves under 0.5 m inside the bay. Choppy conditions are rare, and the lee of La Concha mountain provides additional shelter for coves like Cala del Faro and Cala Cortés.

Typical June Day on the Water

A 4-hour charter (e.g. 10:00–14:00) lets you cruise east from Puerto Banús past the Golden Mile to Marbella Marina, then anchor at Cala del Faro for swimming. Sea conditions are calmest in the morning. An afternoon charter (14:00–18:00) catches the sea breeze at 10–12 knots – ideal for wakeboarding or towed toys. Sunset cruises (18:00–22:00) are increasingly popular: the light softens, the wind drops, and you can watch the sun sink behind La Concha while sipping cava.

All our yachts include a licensed skipper who knows the best anchorages for the day's conditions. They'll adjust the route based on wind and swell.

What to Pack for a June Boat Trip

  • Swimwear and a quick-dry towel
  • Sun hat, UV sunglasses, and SPF 50+ sunscreen (reapply every 2 hours)
  • Light long-sleeve top or rash guard for midday sun
  • Thin jumper or windbreaker for the evening return
  • Non-marking deck shoes or barefoot (no black soles on white decks)
  • Camera or phone – the light is stunning

We provide soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, water, and light snacks. Bring your own food if you prefer; the skipper can recommend a beach restaurant for a lunch stop.

Booking Tips for June

June is the start of peak season in Marbella. Puerto Banús berths fill quickly, and popular yachts like the Azimut 39 (€749/2h) and Mangusta 80 (€4,719/4h) are often booked 3–4 weeks in advance. Weekends are especially busy. Mid-week charters offer more availability and occasionally lower demand.

For the best experience, book a morning or late-afternoon slot to avoid the strongest UV. If you're flexible, ask about the Bandido (11.5m, 8 pax) or Fairline Targa 12m (12m, 10 pax) – both handle June conditions well and are priced on request.

Frequently asked questions

Is June a good month for a boat rental in Marbella?

Yes – June offers the best balance of warm weather, calm seas, and manageable crowds. Air temperatures sit around 26°C, sea water reaches 22°C, and rainfall is virtually zero. The winds are light, typically 10–15 knots from the west, making for smooth cruising along the Costa del Sol.

What should I wear on a boat in Marbella in June?

Light layers work best. Bring swimwear, a sun hat, UV-blocking sunglasses, and a light long-sleeve top for the afternoon sun. Evenings can cool to 20°C, so a thin jumper or windbreaker is useful. Deck shoes with non-marking soles are recommended on all yachts.

How windy is it in Marbella in June?

June winds are generally light to moderate. The prevailing Poniente blows from the west at 10–15 knots, producing a slight sea state (1–2 on the Douglas scale). Choppy conditions are rare, and most days you’ll enjoy flat water within the bay of Marbella and Puerto Banús.

Can I swim from the boat in June?

Yes – the sea temperature averages 22°C, which is comfortable for swimming. Most charters include stops at coves like Cala del Faro or Cala Cortés, where the water is calm and clear. All our boats carry safety equipment and the skipper will choose a sheltered spot.

What time does the sun set in Marbella in June?

Sunset in June is around 21:40, giving you long evenings for sunset cruises. Many guests opt for a 4-hour charter starting at 17:00 to watch the sun dip behind La Concha mountain while enjoying cava and snacks on deck.

Which boat is best for June weather conditions?

For small groups (5 pax), the Dubhe or Mariah SX21 are nimble and perfect for coastal hopping. For larger parties, the Azimut 58 Flybridge offers shaded upper deck and air-conditioned saloon – ideal when UV index hits 9. The Mangusta 80 (24m) handles light chop effortlessly and includes a jet ski.

Do I need to book a June charter in advance?

Strongly recommended. June marks the start of peak season in Marbella, and Puerto Banús berths fill quickly. We suggest booking 3–4 weeks ahead, especially for popular yachts like the Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39, which start at €749 for 2 hours.

What is the water temperature in Marbella in June?

Sea surface temperature averages 22°C in June. It warms from around 20°C at the start of the month to 24°C by late June. This is pleasant for swimming, snorkelling, or paddleboarding without a wetsuit.
