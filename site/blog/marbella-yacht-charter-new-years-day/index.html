Why Choose a New Year's Day Yacht Charter in Marbella?

Marbella in January is mild, calm, and uncrowded. The average temperature sits at 16–18°C, the sea is flat, and the marinas are quiet. A yacht charter in Marbella on New Year's Day lets you skip the tourist crowds and experience the coastline from the water. You'll see the Sierra Blanca mountains dusted with snow, the Golden Mile stretched out behind you, and the iconic yachts of Puerto Banús bobbing at their moorings. It's a peaceful, luxurious start to the year.

Best Routes for a New Year's Day Cruise

Your skipper will tailor the route to the weather, but popular options include:

  • West to Estepona: Cruise past the luxury villas of the Golden Mile, the fishing port of Cabopino, and the rugged coastline to Estepona's marina. About 12 NM each way—perfect for a 4-hour trip.
  • East to Cala del Faro: Head towards Marbella's eastern coves, including the secluded Cala del Faro and Cala Cortés. Anchor for a swim (yes, some brave souls do jump in January).
  • South to the Strait: For longer charters (6–8 hours), venture towards Sotogrande or even the Bay of Gibraltar. Spot dolphins and enjoy open-sea views.

Ask your skipper for recommendations—they know the best spots for the day.

Tips for a Perfect New Year's Day on the Water

  • Dress in layers: 16°C feels cooler on the water. Bring a windproof jacket.
  • Sunscreen: The winter sun can still burn—SPF 30+ is wise.
  • Camera: The light is stunning in January, with low sun and clear skies.
  • Arrive early: Puerto Banús can be quiet, but parking is easy. Aim for 10:00 AM for a morning cruise.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I book a New Year's Day yacht charter in Marbella on short notice?

Yes, we recommend booking at least 48 hours in advance, but last-minute availability is possible. Contact us via WhatsApp to check same-day options. Popular boats like the Azimut 39 and Mangusta 80 fill quickly for New Year's Day.

What is included in the New Year's Day charter price?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, safety equipment, and Spanish IVA (21%). No hidden costs—just show up at Puerto Banús.

How long are New Year's Day charters?

We offer flexible durations from 2 hours to full day (8 hours). The Astondoa 40 and Azimut 39 start at €749 for 2 hours. The Mangusta 80 requires a minimum 4-hour booking at €4,719. For other boats, pricing is on request.

What is the weather like for a New Year's Day boat trip in Marbella?

Marbella enjoys mild winters. New Year's Day typically sees temperatures of 16–18°C with clear skies. The sea is calm, making for a comfortable cruise. Bring a light jacket for the breeze.

Can I bring my own food and drinks on the yacht?

Our standard package includes drinks and light snacks. You are welcome to bring additional food or a picnic, but glass bottles are discouraged for safety. Let us know your preferences when booking.

Which yachts are best for a small group on New Year's Day?

For up to 5 guests, the Dubhe (8m) or Mariah SX21 (6.4m) are ideal. For 6–8 guests, consider the Bandido or Speedboat. Larger groups of 10–12 fit well on the Azimut 58 Flybridge or Mangusta 80.

Is there a minimum age for passengers on New Year's Day charters?

No minimum age, but children must be supervised at all times. Life jackets are available for all sizes. The skipper will brief everyone on safety before departure.

What if the weather is bad on New Year's Day?

We monitor conditions closely. If the forecast is unsafe, we offer a full refund or reschedule to another day. Most New Year's Days in Marbella are sunny and calm.
