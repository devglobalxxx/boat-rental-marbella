Reyes Magos weekend (5-6 January) is one of Spain's most cherished family holidays. In Marbella, the Three Kings' parade lights up the Golden Mile, and the day after, families take to the coast for a relaxed celebration on the water. A Reyes Magos yacht Marbella charter is the perfect way to give your kids a memory that outshines any toy. All our boats depart from Puerto Banús, are fully crewed, and include drinks, snacks, and safety gear – so you just show up and enjoy.

Why charter a yacht for Reyes Magos weekend?

The Epiphany (6 January) is a public holiday across Spain. In Marbella, the Cabalgata de Reyes on the evening of 5 January draws thousands to the streets. The next day, the sea is calm, the marina is quiet, and the sun usually shines at 15-18°C. A 2-hour or 4-hour charter lets you escape the post-parade crowds and explore the Costa del Sol coastline from a new perspective. Kids love spotting the marina's superyachts, watching for dolphins, and anchoring in a sheltered cove for a snack.

Our family-friendly fleet from Puerto Banús

We offer boats for every family size. For a small family of 4-5, the Dubhe (8m, 5 pax) or Mariah SX21 (6.4m, 5 pax) are easy to board and manoeuvre. For larger groups, the Astondoa 40 (12.5m, 9 pax) and Azimut 39 (12.5m, 11 pax) provide plenty of deck space and a cabin for changing. Multi-family groups of up to 12 can book the Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m) or the Mangusta 80 (24m, white or grey). All boats depart from Puerto Banús, just off the Golden Mile.

Best itineraries for families on Reyes Magos weekend

A typical 2-hour charter heads west from Puerto Banús towards Cala del Faro or Cala Cortés, two sheltered coves with calm waters ideal for a short swim stop. For a 4-hour trip, continue to Estepona or Cabopino, where you can anchor for a picnic. The skipper will tailor the route to wind and sea conditions. In January, the sea is usually calm, but we always prioritise safety and comfort for young children.

What to bring for a winter yacht trip with kids

Marbella in January is mild but can be breezy. Pack layers, a windbreaker, sunscreen (yes, the sun reflects off the water), and a hat. Swimwear is optional – the water is around 15°C, so a wetsuit is advisable if you plan to swim. Bring any special Reyes Magos treats or small gifts to surprise the kids on board. The boat has a fridge for your own food if you want to supplement the provided snacks.

Other ways to enjoy Reyes Magos in Marbella

If you're staying for the whole weekend, don't miss the Cabalgata de Reyes on the evening of 5 January. The parade starts near the Golden Mile and winds through the Old Town, ending at the Plaza de los Naranjos with fireworks and gifts. On the 6th, many restaurants offer a special Roscón de Reyes (king cake) brunch. Combine a morning yacht charter with an afternoon on land for a perfect family day.

Frequently asked questions

What is Reyes Magos weekend in Marbella?

Reyes Magos weekend celebrates the Epiphany on 6 January, when the Three Kings bring gifts to children. In Marbella, the main parade (Cabalgata) takes place on the evening of 5 January along the Golden Mile and through Old Town. It's a public holiday, and many families head to the coast for a special day out.

Can we take small children on a yacht charter?

Yes, children are welcome on all our boats. The Dubhe (8m, 5 pax) and Mariah SX21 (6.4m, 5 pax) are great for small families. Life jackets are provided for all ages, and the skipper ensures a safe, calm ride. We recommend the 2-hour charter for young kids to keep it fun and relaxed.

Which boat is best for a family of 5?

The Dubhe (8m, 5 pax) is perfect for a family of 5. It's easy to board, has a shaded area, and departs from Puerto Banús. For a bit more space, the Mariah SX21 (6.4m, 5 pax) offers a sportier feel. Both are priced on request, so message us on WhatsApp for a quote.

What is included in the charter price for families?

Every charter includes a licensed skipper, fuel, water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, light snacks, insurance, and safety equipment. The 21% Spanish VAT is also included. No hidden extras. For Tier A boats (Astondoa 40, Azimut 39), the 2-hour charter is €749 all in.

How do I book a yacht for Reyes Magos weekend?

Booking is simple: contact us via WhatsApp (link on our site) with your preferred boat, date, and number of guests. We'll confirm availability and send payment details. For the Reyes Magos weekend (5-6 January), we recommend booking at least a week in advance as slots fill quickly.

Are there any special Reyes Magos activities on the water?

While we don't run themed events, you can bring your own small gifts or treats for the kids to enjoy on board. Many families anchor near Cala del Faro or Cala Cortés for a swim and a picnic. The skipper can suggest quiet coves away from the crowds.

What is the weather like in Marbella during early January?

Marbella in January averages 14-18°C daytime, with clear skies and calm seas. The water temperature is around 15°C, so swimming is possible with a wetsuit. For a winter charter, bring layers and a windbreaker – the sun is still strong, but the breeze can be cool.

Can we charter a larger boat for a multi-family group?

Absolutely. The Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m, 12 pax) and Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) are ideal for two or three families. The Mangusta 80 starts at €4,719 for 4 hours, and includes a free jet ski. For exact pricing, message us on WhatsApp.
