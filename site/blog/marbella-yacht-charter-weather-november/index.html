Planning a Marbella yacht charter in November? You’re in for a treat. The summer crowds have gone, the sea is still warm enough for a dip, and the light over La Concha is stunning. November brings mild temperatures, calm seas, and a relaxed pace – ideal for a private day on the water. This guide covers everything you need to know about the weather, what to pack, and how to make the most of your charter.

November Weather Overview for Marbella

November in Marbella is mild and mostly pleasant. Average daytime highs hover around 18°C, dropping to 11°C at night. You’ll get about 6 hours of sunshine per day, with sunset around 18:00. Rainfall averages 60 mm spread over 6–8 days, usually as short showers. The sea temperature sits at 16–18°C – refreshing but not cold. Wind is typically 10–15 knots from the west, with seas generally calm to slight. Swell rarely exceeds 1 m, especially in sheltered bays like Cala del Faro or Cala Cortés.

Sea Conditions and Sailing Comfort

The Costa del Sol is known for its sheltered coastline, and November is no exception. The mountains behind Marbella block much of the wind, so conditions near shore are often flat. For longer trips towards Estepona or Sotogrande, a light chop may appear in the afternoon. Our skippers know the local conditions and will choose the best route – whether that’s cruising past the Golden Mile or anchoring off Cabopino. All our boats are equipped with stabilisers or deep-V hulls for a smooth ride.

What to Wear and Pack for November

Layers are your best friend. Start with a T-shirt or light sweater, add a windproof jacket or fleece, and bring a pair of sunglasses. Non-slip shoes are recommended for boarding. If you plan to swim, a wetsuit or rash vest helps with the 16–18°C sea temperature. Sunscreen is still useful – the UV index is moderate but can catch you out. A hat and a scarf for the evening breeze are smart additions.

Suggested Itineraries for November Days

With shorter days, a half-day charter works well. Depart Puerto Banús at 14:00, cruise east past the Marbella Marina and Cabopino, anchor at Cala del Faro for a swim and snacks, then return by 18:00 as the sun sets behind La Concha. Alternatively, head west towards Estepona and anchor off the coast for a relaxed afternoon. For a full-day trip, you can reach Sotogrande or even Gibraltar, but plan for a 10:00 start to maximise daylight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I still swim during a November yacht charter in Marbella?

Yes, but the sea temperature is 16–18°C. Most people find it refreshing but brief. A wetsuit or light jacket is advisable. Many guests prefer to enjoy the coastline views and onboard amenities instead.

What should I wear for a November boat trip in Marbella?

Layers are key. Start with a T-shirt or light sweater, bring a windproof jacket or fleece, and wear comfortable trousers or shorts. Non-slip shoes are recommended. The sun can still be strong midday, so sunglasses and sunscreen are useful.

Is November a good time for a private yacht charter in Marbella?

Absolutely. November offers mild weather, fewer crowds, and more flexible booking. You’ll enjoy the same stunning coastline with quieter anchorages. Our all-inclusive charters include a skipper, drinks, and snacks – perfect for a relaxed day out.

What is the wind like in November in Marbella?

Typical winds are 10–15 knots from the west, occasionally stronger in the afternoon. The coast is sheltered by the Sierra Blanca mountains, so conditions near shore are often calm. Our skippers adjust the route to ensure a comfortable ride.

Which boats are best for November weather?

Flybridge boats like the Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m, 12 pax) offer a covered upper deck, ideal for cooler days. The Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) has spacious saloons. For smaller groups, the Astondoa 40 (12.5m, 9 pax) is cosy and affordable.

What is the sunset time in Marbella in November?

Sunset is around 18:00, so afternoon charters starting at 14:00 or 15:00 let you enjoy the golden hour. Evening cruises are popular – the sky turns orange over La Concha mountain.

Is it rainy in Marbella during November?

November sees about 60 mm of rain over 6–8 days, usually as short showers. Many days are dry and bright. We monitor forecasts closely and can reschedule if needed, though cancellations are rare.
