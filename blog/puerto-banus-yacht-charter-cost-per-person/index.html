Planning a group day on the water in Marbella? The Puerto Banús yacht charter cost per person is the smartest way to budget. Instead of guessing, we break down exactly what you’ll pay per head — from €83 for a quick spin to €192 for a full-day luxury escape. No hidden fees, no surprises. Just clear numbers on our fleet of 18+ boats departing from Puerto Banús.

How the Per-Person Cost Works

Every charter we offer includes a licensed skipper, fuel, drinks (water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava), light snacks, insurance, and 21% Spanish IVA. The price you see is the price you pay — just divide by your group size. For example, an 8-hour charter on the Azimut 39 at €2,299 split among 11 people equals €209 each. No extra charges for fuel or VAT.

Puerto Banús Yacht Charter Cost Per Person: Tier A Boats

Our Tier A fleet includes the Astondoa 40 (12.5m, 9 pax) and Azimut 39 (12.5m, 11 pax). These are the most affordable options per person:

  • 2-hour charter: €749 total → €83/person (9 pax) or €68/person (11 pax).
  • 4-hour charter: €1,399 total → €155/person (9 pax) or €127/person (11 pax).
  • 8-hour charter: €2,299 total → €255/person (9 pax) or €209/person (11 pax).

These are ideal for small groups wanting a taste of the Marbella coastline without breaking the bank. Departures are from Puerto Banús, with routes to Cabopino, Estepona, or Cala del Faro.

Luxury Yacht Per-Person Costs: Mangusta 80 and Azimut 58

For larger groups (up to 12 pax), the Mangusta 80 (24m, 12 pax) and Azimut 58 Flybridge (17.6m, 12 pax) offer premium space and speed. The Mangusta 80 starts at €4,719 for a minimum 4-hour charter — that’s €393 per person for 12 people, and includes a free jet ski. The Azimut 58 Flybridge is priced on request via WhatsApp, but expect a similar per-person range for full-day bookings.

These boats are perfect for cruising past the Golden Mile, anchoring at Cala Cortés, or lunching at a beach club in Sotogrande.

Cost Per Person for Other Boats in Our Fleet

We have over a dozen additional boats, from the Ferretti 94 (29m, 12 pax) to the K80 (24m, 12 pax) and the Canados 86 (26m, 12 pax). Pricing for these is on request via WhatsApp because each charter is customised. However, as a rule of thumb, full-day (8h) charters on these superyachts typically range from €300–€600 per person for 12 guests, depending on the season and itinerary.

Smaller boats like the Speedboat (7m, 6 pax) or Mariah SX21 (6.4m, 5 pax) are ideal for intimate groups — pricing on request, but expect €150–€250 per person for a half-day.

What’s Included in the Per-Person Price?

Every charter includes:

  • Licensed skipper and crew
  • Fuel for the entire trip
  • Water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava
  • Light snacks (chips, olives, nuts)
  • Full insurance and safety equipment
  • Spanish IVA (21%) — no hidden tax

The only optional extra is a gratuity for the crew (typically 10–15%). You can also bring your own food or arrange catering through us.

How to Reduce Your Per-Person Cost Further

To get the lowest Puerto Banús yacht charter cost per person, book a boat with maximum capacity. For example, the Azimut 39 fits 11 people — cheaper per head than a 9-pax boat. Also, opt for a half-day (4h) instead of full-day (8h) if you’re on a tight budget. Last-minute weekday bookings sometimes have availability, but we recommend booking 1–2 weeks ahead during summer (June–September).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest yacht charter in Puerto Banús per person?

The cheapest per-person rate is on our Astondoa 40 or Azimut 39, starting at €749 for 2 hours. With 9 passengers, that’s €83 per person. For 8 hours, the same boat costs €2,299, or €192 per person for 12 passengers.

Are drinks and food included in the per-person cost?

Yes, every charter includes a selection of water, soft drinks, beer, white wine, cava, and light snacks. There’s no extra charge for these — they’re part of the quoted price. You’re welcome to bring your own food or order catering via the crew.

Does the per-person price include fuel and the skipper?

Absolutely. The skipper’s fee, fuel, insurance, and 21% Spanish IVA are all included in the published rate. There are no surprise fees when you split the cost with your group.

How many people can fit on a boat for the best per-person rate?

For the lowest per-person cost, book a boat that fits 11–12 passengers, like the Azimut 39 (11 pax) or Astondoa 40 (9 pax). The Azimut 58 Flybridge and Mangusta 80 accommodate 12 pax, giving you an even lower per-person split.

What’s the per-person cost for a luxury yacht like the Mangusta 80?

The Mangusta 80 starts at €4,719 for a minimum 4-hour charter, which includes a free jet ski. With 12 passengers, that’s €393 per person for 4 hours. For longer charters, pricing is available on request via WhatsApp.

Are there any hidden fees when splitting the cost?

No hidden fees. The quoted price includes skipper, fuel, drinks, snacks, insurance, and VAT. The only optional extra is a gratuity for the crew, typically 10–15% of the charter fee. You can also bring your own food or order catering.

Can I charter a boat for a half-day to save per person?

Yes, half-day charters (4 hours) are available on most boats. For example, a 4-hour charter on the Astondoa 40 costs €1,399 (€156/person for 9 pax). The Mangusta 80 requires a minimum 4-hour booking, starting at €4,719.

What’s the best value per person for a full-day charter?

The best full-day value is on the Azimut 39 or Astondoa 40 at €2,299 for 8 hours. With 11–12 passengers, that’s €192–€209 per person. For a larger group, the Azimut 58 Flybridge or Mangusta 80 (12 pax) offers luxury at a similar per-person rate.
